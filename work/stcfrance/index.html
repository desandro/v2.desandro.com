STC France Logo comps round 1

Here is the first round of logo ideas for your group. I tried to take all the considerations into account: Make it work at small sizes for avatars; Limited use of color for less expensive printing; Identify with France; Identify with Technical Communication. I've provided several variations of each comp, just to give an idea of other options that we could pursue.

Comp 1 - Eiffel

As requested, this logo incorporates the Eiffel Tower. I wanted to use the shape of the tower in the negative space so it becomes less prominent. This way it becomes the last thing you notice about the logo. You first read STC in cursive, then "France," and then you're eye catches the tower. The Eiffel Tower is such an icon, I didn't want to use it straight forward, since it might dominate the identity.

Comp 2 - Letters

This logo took a more straight forward approach of using the shapes of the letters. The curves of the S and C are perfect circles. I decided to use these basic shapes to mimic technical diagrams, the sort of illustrations engineers would use. I felt this was appropriate, as opposed to more expressive or evocative shapes. I like the top variation the best because the propotions work well. The width of the letter strokes is the same size of the width of the counters and negative space.

There are two treatments. One with transparency - you could think of these as Venn diagrams. One with the T as purely negative space. You can also see how the logo would look in black and white.

Comp 3 - Bubbles

This logo was a bit more experimental. I was interested in seeing I could fit the letters in to one another. Again, I used the blue, white, and red of the French flag.

Comp 4 - Blocky

The goal with these logos was to make the letters more abstract, but still use simple shapes. Stretching out the right side gives the shapes some character, and also mimic the traditional french flag (rotated 90 degrees). The top variation has is slanted to give it some motion. The right versions have a 3D rotation applied, to make them more dynamic.
